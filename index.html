OpenAI: a practical guide to building agents
OpenAI · 2026
Key takeaway
OpenAI's design principle is "give yourself all the upside and cap the downside" — rather than predicting how often a model will get it right, design agents so failures are bounded. They argue strongly for starting with single-agent loops (one model + tools + clear instructions) and only adding complexity once the simple version is working. The bounded-blast-radius mental model is one of the most senior frameworks any PM can adopt for AI feature design: instead of trying to predict model accuracy, design so failures stay contained, and apply this principle to every AI feature you propose.
