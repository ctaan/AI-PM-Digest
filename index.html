Lenny's Newsletter · June 28, 2026
Key takeaway
Ambrosino describes a "zone defense" model for PMs at OpenAI, where instead of owning a fixed backlog, a PM covers an area of the product and anyone — including the PM — builds directly when a gap opens, with taste emerging as the most valuable individual skill as building gets cheap. He's explicit that eliminating the PM role entirely, even as responsibilities blur, is a mistake. Practice this by picking one small feature this month and prototyping it yourself instead of only writing the requirements doc.

The hidden pattern behind successful products | Mark Pincus (founder of Zynga)
Lenny's Newsletter · June 14, 2026
Key takeaway
Pincus's "Proven, Better, New" framework argues hit products copy something already proven, make it meaningfully better (his bar: 10 out of 10 test users say an unqualified yes), then add exactly one new element — rather than chasing originality first. He pairs this with a rule that your instincts are right roughly 95% of the time but your specific ideas are wrong about 75% of the time. Apply this principle to AI feature ideation specifically: use AI to rapidly prototype variations on a proven pattern and test for the unanimous "yes" reaction before layering in the genuinely novel piece, rather than asking AI to generate novelty from a blank page.

The new inner game: Your unfair advantage in the age of AI
Lenny's Newsletter · June 23, 2026
Key takeaway
Joe Hudson, who coaches OpenAI's research team, argues that as AI commoditizes knowledge and effort, the scarce differentiator becomes emotional clarity — discernment, willingness to surface conflict, comfort with fast failure, and non-hostile self-talk. For PM career development, the practical takeaway is to treat pace (how fast you iterate) and spin (self-doubt and hesitation before shipping) as trackable personal metrics, not just team ones.
