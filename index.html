Key takeaway
Willison argues the frontier labs hit real product-market fit not with chatbots but with coding and general-purpose agents priced at enterprise API rates — power users now burn roughly $1,000/month in tokens, and the best pricing makes customers "suck air through their teeth and then say yes." The non-obvious insight for PMs: the durable AI business model is high-token, high-value professional work, not $10–20/month consumer subscriptions — and a coding agent is really "anything you can do by typing commands into a computer," so the addressable surface of knowledge work is enormous. Apply this when you price or scope an AI feature: anchor value to the expensive human work it replaces, and design for usage-based economics rather than flat seats.
